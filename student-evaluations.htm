Student Evaluations

Student evaluations for ECH 4605 - Product & Process Systems Engineering and ECH 4615 - Product & Process Design have been provided for Fall 2017, Spring 2018, and Fall 2018 semesters.

The categories I chose to focus on, in alignment with my teaching philosophy, are:

  • E2. Communication of Ideas and Information
  • E3. Expression of Expectations for Performance
  • E8. Overall Rating of the Instructor

As can be seen, there is a strong correlation between how students rated communication and expression of expectations with how well they rated the overall instructor performance. When students are given clear goals for achieving in a course—even if the problem is open-ended—they will enjoy the course and often work harder to meet the instructor's expectations.

There is also a noticeable difference between ratings for ECH 4605 and ECH 4615 courses. These two courses are taught back-to-back in sequence, and are colloquially referred to as "Design I" and "Design II". Historically, they have been taught by the same instructor team for both semesters. It is clear from the 2017-2018 school year, that students matriculating to Design II tend to be much happier with the coursework. This is because the instruction is focused more on group work, where student groups will have weekly and biweekly meetings with TAs to discuss their project progress. These meetings are integral for student-instructor communication and offer us a way to guide students toward a working solution for their design.
